### "Tell me about a time you had to manage a difficult client."

**Story (from your experience):**

"During the NIS2 rollout, I was working with a client where legal, IT, and operations had conflicting interpretations of NIS2 scope and obligations.

Legal wanted minimal disclosure. IT wanted full transparency. Operations didn't want to be slowed down by compliance.

My role was to facilitate alignment. I organized a series of workshops with all stakeholders, walked through NIS2 requirements systematically, and showed them the consequences of each interpretation – both regulatory and operational.

I brought in external legal counsel to clarify ambiguities, and I created a decision matrix that mapped each interpretation to risk, cost, and timeline.

Within 3 weeks, we had consensus. We went with a balanced approach: transparent internally, selective disclosure externally, and a phased rollout that didn't block operations.

The result was full NIS2 compliance across 10 countries, and the client asked us to extend the engagement for ISO 27001 integration."

### "How do you build and lead teams?"

**Antwort:**

"I've led teams from 10 to 100+ people, both as direct reports and in matrix/project structures.

My approach is simple: clarity, empowerment, and feedback.

**Clarity:** Everyone knows their role, responsibilities, and success metrics. No ambiguity. For example, in the GRC program at Capgemini, I had 15 projects and 30+ consultants. I created a RACI matrix and weekly sync rituals so everyone knew where they stood.

**Empowerment:** I hire smart people and trust them. I don't micromanage. I set the direction, remove blockers, and let them execute. In the IAM/PAM program, I had a team across Germany and Poland. I gave them autonomy and accountability.

**Feedback:** Regular 1-on-1s, performance reviews, and recognition. When someone does great work, I tell them and their leadership. When there's underperformance, I address it directly and support improvement.

I also invest in development. At sg services, I mentored juniors into project leads. At MHP, I'm training the team on NIS2 and ISO 21434 so they can deliver independently.

The result? Low turnover, high performance, and people who want to work with me."
